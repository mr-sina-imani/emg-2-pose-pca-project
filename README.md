# Code for the PCA on EMG2POSE Project

## Abstract
Estimating the human hand posture has many applications, from virtual reality applications to remote critical operations or rehabilitation of people with muscle weakness. Among them, one of the notable technologies is the use of the Elegtromyography signal at the skin surface; because this signal originates from the neural activity caused by muscle stimulation. Given the real-time and continuous nature of some of these applications, the question arises whether it is possible to reduce the volume of the model by some amount, regardless of its performance, in such a way that its computational cost and energy requirements are reduced. In this project, by using the redundancy in the anatomy of the hand, we describe its position with a smaller vector and thus predict the hand posture with a smaller model. This research path, if developed, could lead to a lower economic and environmental cost for this method.
